# Tier 1 Pre-Launch: Human-Required Tests

These three items from the Tier 1 list require you (Brad) to run them with real people. Use this doc to brief yourself or anyone helping you run them.

---

## 1. The 5-Second Test

**What it is:** Show the hero section of sellwise.au to 5 strangers for exactly 5 seconds, then cover it. Ask them what they just saw.

**What you're measuring:** Can someone who has never heard of SellWise explain what it does after 5 seconds? If they can't, the hero needs more work.

**How to run it:**

1. Open sellwise.au on your phone or laptop
2. Show the screen for 5 seconds to someone who hasn't seen it (friend, partner, colleague)
3. Cover the screen immediately
4. Ask these three questions in order — do NOT lead them:
   - "What does that website do?"
   - "Who is it for?"
   - "What would you do if you landed on that page?"
5. Write down exactly what they say, word for word

**Minimum:** 5 people. Mix of: people who sell online, people who don't.

**Pass criteria:** At least 4 of 5 can correctly say "it helps sellers write listings" or similar. If they say "AI tool" or "SEO" that's also fine. If they say "e-commerce website" or "marketplace" — the hero has failed.

**When to do it:** As soon as the coming soon page is replaced with the live landing page, or share the URL directly with people now.

## 2. Mom Test Interviews

**What they are:** 5 conversations with real online sellers, using Rob Fitzpatrick's Mom Test principles. The goal is to uncover real pain — not to validate SellWise.

**The Mom Test rules (read these first):**
- Ask about their life, not your idea
- Never pitch SellWise during the interview — just listen
- Ask about the past, not hypothetical futures ("Did you ever…" not "Would you ever…")
- If they say something nice about your idea, dig into the behaviour: "When was the last time you actually did that?"

**Who to interview:** Sellers on Shopify, eBay, or Etsy. Mix of experience levels. At least one who has paid for a seller tool before.

**Interview script (20 minutes):**

Opening (don't mention SellWise):
> "I'm researching how online sellers manage their listings. Mind if I ask you a few questions about your experience? There's no pitch at the end — I just want to understand how it actually works for you."

Questions (pick 6-8, don't rush through all of them):

1. "How long have you been selling on [platform]?"
2. "Walk me through the last listing you wrote from scratch. What did that look like?"
3. "How long did it take you?"
4. "What did you do to try to rank it or get it found?"
5. "Have you ever changed a listing after it was live? What made you do that?"
6. "What's the most frustrating part of managing your listings?"
7. "Have you ever paid for any tools to help with your listings or SEO? Which ones? Are you still using them?"
8. "What made you stop using it / keep using it?" (follow-up)
9. "If you had to spend 2 hours improving your store this week, where would you start?"
10. "What does 'a listing that's working' look like for you? How do you know?"

Closing:
> "Last question — is there anything about writing or managing listings that you wish someone would just solve for you?"

**What to listen for:**
- Specific pain phrases (write these down verbatim — they're your copywriting gold)
- What they currently use and pay for
- How much time they spend on listings
- The emotional undercurrent (do they feel confident, frustrated, resigned?)

**After each interview:** Write up 3-5 bullet points immediately while it's fresh. Look for patterns across all 5.

## 3. Unmoderated Usability Test

**What it is:** Give 5 people a task and watch them do it. You don't explain anything. You just watch where they get confused.

**The task to give them:**
> "You're an Etsy seller. You make handmade jewellery. Use this website to optimise this product listing: [give them a real product title and short description to paste in]. I'll be quiet while you do it — just think out loud as you go."

**Prep a test listing first.** Something like:
- Product name: "Sterling Silver Initial Necklace"
- Description: "Handmade sterling silver necklace with initial charm. 18 inch chain. Gift box included."
- Category: Jewellery / Necklaces

**How to run it:**
1. Give them a laptop with sellwise.au open (or share screen on Zoom and hand them control)
2. Read the task out loud, hand them the product details
3. Stay silent. If they get stuck and ask for help, say: "What would you do if I wasn't here?"
4. Watch for: hesitation, re-reading labels, clicking wrong things, confusion about which platform to pick, not knowing what to do with the result

**What to note:**
- Where do they pause for more than 3 seconds?
- What do they read vs skip?
- What do they click that doesn't work how they expected?
- What's the first thing they say when they see the result?
- What do they do AFTER they see the result? (Do they know what to do next?)

**Who to test with:** 5 people who sell online, OR people who are reasonably tech-literate (don't test with people who struggle with tech — that's a different problem). Mix of Shopify/eBay/Etsy sellers is ideal.

**Minimum pass criteria:** At least 4 of 5 complete a first optimisation without asking for help. If fewer than that succeed, prioritise the friction points before launch.

## What to do with the results

After running all three:

1. **5-second test failures** → brief Claude Code with the exact words people used + what they misunderstood → we'll rewrite accordingly
2. **Interview pain phrases** → brief Claude Code with verbatim quotes from sellers → we'll use them in landing page and email copy
3. **Usability blockers** → brief Claude Code with exactly where people got stuck → we'll fix the UI

The best outcomes from these tests are: a headline that uses a seller's own words, copy that names the exact frustration they described, and a flow with zero hesitation moments.
