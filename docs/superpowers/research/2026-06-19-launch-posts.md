## 6. Indie Hackers — story post

**Title:** I built SellWise: AI that writes marketplace listings for online sellers — here's what 6 weeks of nights and weekends looked like

**Body:**

**The problem I was trying to solve:**

I run a small online store alongside my full-time job. Writing product listings properly — titles, descriptions, meta fields, tags — was taking me 45 minutes per listing when I did it right. When I rushed it, the listings didn't rank.

I tried ChatGPT. The copy was fine but it had no concept of platform-specific rules. It didn't know eBay titles max out at 80 characters, or that Amazon penalises keyword repetition in backend fields, or that Etsy's algorithm reads the first 160 characters of your description before anything else. Readable copy is not the same as copy that ranks.

**What I built:**

SellWise is an AI listing writer that knows each marketplace's SEO rules. You paste your product details, pick your platform (Shopify, eBay, Amazon, Etsy), and get a fully formatted listing in about 30 seconds.

Every result gets a score from 0 to 100 based on how well it follows the platform's algorithm requirements — banned words, keyword coverage, tag uniqueness, title/description overlap. If your score is below 60, it shows you exactly what's dragging it down and lets you fix it in one click.

**Tech stack:**

- Next.js 16, React 19, Tailwind 4, TypeScript
- Supabase for auth and database
- Stripe for billing
- Claude Sonnet for AI (platform-aware prompts, structured JSON output)
- Vercel for deployment

Built it entirely with Claude Code over about 6 weeks of evenings and weekends. My background is in electrical estimating, not software — this was a genuine learning project.

**What I learned:**

The scoring system was the hardest part to get right. Early versions just returned a number with no explanation. Users had no idea what to do with an 82 or a 54. The breakthrough was breaking the score into specific deductions with actionable labels — "Banned word used: beautiful. Replace with specific language." That made it immediately useful.

The other big lesson: platform-specific prompts matter a lot. A single generic prompt for all four marketplaces produced mediocre results everywhere. Separate prompts tuned to each platform's requirements made a noticeable difference in output quality.

**Current status:**

7-day free trial at sellwise.au, no card required. Shopify and eBay store connect are fully built (OAuth, push content back to the live store). Manual optimiser works for all four platforms.

Looking for early users, especially Shopify and eBay sellers. Would love to hear what your current listing workflow looks like.
